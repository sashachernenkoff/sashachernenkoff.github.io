hi, my name is sasha chernenkoff!

i graduated from the University of Pittsburgh in 2020 with a BS in chemistry, and now work as a research & development scientist at PerkinElmer Genomics, where i develop second-tier biochemical tests for the diagnosis of lysosomal storage disorders in newborn screening algorithms. working at PerkinElmer has helped elucidate my love for genomics and bioinformatics! now, i am particularly interested in how machine learning can improve the diagnostic potential of genome sequencing. i am a prospective bioinformatics MS student, whose research focuses on variant interpretation models.

i can generally be found behind my computer screen writing code or playing games, getting lost in the canadian rockies with my dogs, looking at infrastructure with my civil engineer husband, or designing and building websites, keyboards, and graphics!

feel free to have a look through my work! i love talking about my projects, the outdoors, keyboards, or dogs. whether you are looking for a research collaborator or hiking partner, do not hesitate to reach out via email!
